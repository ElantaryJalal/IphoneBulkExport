Why it’s different

It reads your phone’s actual photo library — the same way iMazing does — so nothing gets missed, and you stay in control.

📂

Your whole library

Reads Photos.sqlite for every asset with real capture dates — not just the DCIM folder Windows shows you.

✅

Pick exactly what you want

A checkable list with date and type filters. Export everything, or just your 2024 videos, or a hand-picked set.

🔁

Resumable

Already-copied files are skipped. If a transfer stops, just run it again — it continues where it left off.

🖼️

Keep or convert

Keep originals (HEIC/MOV), or convert to universal JPG and MP4 on the way out.

☁️

Honest about iCloud

Photos stored only in iCloud aren’t on the phone — the app lists them so you know exactly what to download.

🔌

No cloud, no account

Everything happens locally over the USB cable. Your photos never touch a server.

Three steps

Plug in, choose, export.

Connect & trust

Plug the iPhone in, unlock it, tap Trust. The status light turns green.

Scan & choose

Pick a folder, hit Scan & Choose, then tick the photos/videos you want.

Export

Watch the progress bar. Originals or JPG/MP4 — your call. Done.

💡 For big libraries, set the iPhone’s Auto-Lock to Never while it runs — a locked phone interrupts the USB connection.

What you need

  • Windows 10 or 11 (64-bit).
  • The free Apple Devices app (or iTunes) from the Microsoft Store — it installs the USB driver. You don’t have to open it.
  • A data-capable USB cable (the charging cable works).
  • Enough free disk space for your photos (the app shows the total before it starts).
Optional: install the free HEIF Image Extensions from the Store to preview .HEIC photos in Windows — or just use the “Convert to JPG” option.

FAQ

Windows says “Windows protected your PC” when I open it

That’s SmartScreen warning about a new, unsigned app — normal for indie tools. Click More info → Run anyway. The app is open source; you can read or build it yourself.

Do I need iTunes or an iCloud account?

No iCloud account. You do need the Apple USB driver, which comes from the free Apple Devices app (or iTunes) in the Microsoft Store. The app never signs in anywhere.

Some photos didn’t copy

Those are stored only in iCloud (with “Optimize iPhone Storage” on), so they aren’t physically on the phone — no USB tool can fetch them. The app lists them in not_on_device.txt. Turn on Settings → Photos → Download and Keep Originals, let it sync, then run again.

Is it safe? Where do my photos go?

Straight from the cable to the folder you choose — nothing is uploaded. The code is open source on GitHub.
